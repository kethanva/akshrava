# Supervised Trial Protocol

This document outlines the protocol for conducting supervised trials of the Akshrava platform with blind or low-vision users.

## 1. Objective
To safely test the efficacy of the vision assistance system in a controlled, real-world environment while ensuring user safety.

## 2. Participant Briefing
- Explain that Akshrava is an experimental assistance tool and NOT a replacement for a white cane or guide dog.
- Instruct the user to **always rely on their primary mobility aid** (cane or dog).
- Explain the types of alerts they will hear ("Obstacle ahead", "Vehicle nearby").
- Demonstrate the visible Start and Stop controls and the foreground-service notification.
- Do not promise headset-button controls, a priority look mode, or offline obstacle detection:
  none is enabled in the current build.

## 3. Environment Selection
- **Phase 1 (Controlled)**: A quiet, enclosed park or empty parking lot with placed obstacles (chairs, boxes, parked cars).
- **Phase 2 (Semi-Controlled)**: A quiet residential street with low traffic and clear sidewalks.
- **Phase 3 (Supervised Real-World)**: A typical street with moderate pedestrian traffic.

## 4. Safety Protocol
- **Supervisor Role**: The supervisor must walk closely behind or beside the user at all times.
- **Intervention**: The supervisor MUST intervene immediately if the user is about to collide with an object, step into traffic, or if the system fails to warn them of a hazard.
- **Kinematic Honesty**: The system will never say "safe to cross". The supervisor must verbally confirm it is safe to cross streets during the trial.

## 5. Data Collection
- Record the user's reaction times to alerts.
- Note any false positives or missed hazards.
- Note battery drain and thermal performance of the device during the trial.
- Conduct a post-trial interview to gather user feedback on alert frequency, volume, and helpfulness.
